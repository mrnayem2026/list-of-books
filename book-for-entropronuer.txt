Sure, here is a list of books that are considered beneficial for entrepreneurs:

* **Zero to One: Notes on Startups, or How to Build the Future by Peter Thiel** [Image of Zero to One: Notes on Startups, or How to Build the Future by Peter Thiel] 
This book challenges entrepreneurs to focus on creating new value (innovation) instead of just copying what exists (competition). 

* **The Lean Startup by Eric Ries** [Image of The Lean Startup by Eric Ries] 
This book advocates for building a minimum viable product (MVP) and using customer feedback to iterate and improve your product or service quickly.

* **Start with Why: How Great Leaders Inspire Everyone to Take Action by Simon Sinek** [Image of Start with Why: How Great Leaders Inspire Everyone to Take Action by Simon Sinek] 
This book emphasizes the importance of having a clear vision and purpose for your business. 

* **Shoe Dog: A Memoir by the Creator of Nike by Phil Knight** [Image of Shoe Dog: A Memoir by the Creator of Nike by Phil Knight] 
This memoir by Phil Knight, the co-founder of Nike, chronicles the early days of the company and the challenges he faced along the way. It's a great source of inspiration and practical advice for aspiring entrepreneurs.

* **The Hard Thing About Hard Things: Building a Business When There Are No Easy Answers by Ben Horowitz** [Image of The Hard Thing About Hard Things: Building a Business When There Are No Easy Answers by Ben Horowitz] 
This book offers realistic advice on the challenges of building a startup, including dealing with fundraising, hiring, and managing a team.

* **The E-Myth Revisited: Why Most Small Businesses Don't Work and What to Do About It by Michael E. Gerber** [Image of The E-Myth Revisited: Why Most Small Businesses Don't Work and What to Do About It by Michael E. Gerber] 
This book argues that most small businesses are run by technicians who are passionate about their craft but lack the business skills necessary for success. Gerber provides a framework for building a business that systems-driven and scalable.

* **Traction: Get a Grip on Your Business by Gabriel Weinberg** [Image of Traction: Get a Grip on Your Business by Gabriel Weinberg] 
This book  focuses on the 19 different channels you can use to get customers and how to figure out which ones will work best for your business.

* **Crush It! Why NOW Is the Time to Cash In on Your Passion by Gary Vaynerchuk** [Image of Crush It! Why NOW Is the Time to Cash In on Your Passion by Gary Vaynerchuk] 
This book argues that the internet has made it easier than ever to turn your passion into a profitable business. Vaynerchuk provides practical advice on how to use social media and other online tools to market yourself and your business.

* **The $100 Startup by Chris Guillebeau** [Image of The $100 Startup by Chris Guillebeau] 
This book argues that you don't need a lot of money to start a business. Guillebeau provides a number of low-cost business ideas and strategies.

* **Think and Grow Rich by Napoleon Hill** [Image of Think and Grow Rich by Napoleon Hill] 
This classic book outlines 13 principles of success that can be applied to any area of life, including business.

This is just a starting point, of course, and there are many other great books out there for entrepreneurs. The best book for you will depend on your specific stage of business and your individual needs.
