Here are some popular personal development books:

* **Atomic Habits by James Clear**  [Image of Atomic Habits by James Clear]
This book is a great introduction to the science of habit formation and how you can use it to build good habits and break bad ones. Clear's Four Laws of Behavior Change - Make it Obvious, Make it Attractive, Make it Easy, and Make it Satisfying - are a practical framework that can be applied to any area of your life.

* **Mindset: The New Psychology of Success by Carol S. Dweck** [Image of Mindset: The New Psychology of Success by Carol S. Dweck]
This book explores the difference between a fixed mindset and a growth mindset. A fixed mindset believes that your talents and abilities are set in stone, while a growth mindset believes that you can learn and improve with effort. Dweck shows how having a growth mindset can lead to greater success in all areas of life.

* **The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People by Stephen Covey** [Image of The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People by Stephen Covey]
This classic book outlines seven habits that can help you become more effective in all areas of your life. Covey's habits are based on timeless principles that are still relevant today.

* **How to Win Friends and Influence People by Dale Carnegie** [Image of How to Win Friends and Influence People by Dale Carnegie]
This book is a classic guide to improving your communication and interpersonal skills. Carnegie's principles are simple but effective, and they can help you build stronger relationships with the people in your life.

* **Daring Greatly: How the Courage to Be Vulnerable Transforms the Way We Live, Love, Parent, and Lead by Brené Brown** [Image of Daring Greatly: How the Courage to Be Vulnerable Transforms the Way We Live, Love, Parent, and Lead by Brené Brown]
This book explores the power of vulnerability. Brown argues that vulnerability is the key to building strong relationships and living a more authentic life.

* **Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman** [Image of Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman]
This book explores the two different ways that we think: System 1, which is fast and automatic, and System 2, which is slow and deliberate. Kahneman shows how our thinking can be biased and how we can make better decisions by being aware of these biases.

* **Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World by Cal Newport** [Image of Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World by Cal Newport]
This book argues that the ability to focus on deep work - professional activities that produce our best outcomes - is becoming increasingly rare and valuable. Newport provides strategies for protecting your time and improving your ability to focus.

* **The Power of Now: A Guide to Spiritual Enlightenment by Eckhart Tolle** [Image of The Power of Now: A Guide to Spiritual Enlightenment by Eckhart Tolle]
This book teaches you how to focus on the present moment and let go of negative thoughts and emotions. Tolle's teachings can help you to find peace and happiness in your life.

* **The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck by Mark Manson** [Image of The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck by Mark Manson]
This book is a counterintuitive take on personal development. Manson argues that the key to happiness is not trying to be positive all the time, but rather to accept that some things are outside of our control and to focus on the things that we can control. 

* **Essentialism: The Disciplined Pursuit of Less by Greg McKeown** [Image of Essentialism: The Disciplined Pursuit of Less by Greg McKeown]
This book teaches you how to identify the essential things in your life and eliminate the non-essential things. McKeown's approach can help you to be more productive and to live a more meaningful life.

I hope this list helps you find a book that can help you on your personal development journey!
